Case Study · Medicare Client Demographics (Power BI)

Data privacy: Client identities are anonymized. For public hosting, replace visuals with screenshots from a synthetic/public dataset that mirrors the structure.

Overview

View PDF Export

This dashboard was created in Power BI to give leadership visibility into our Medicare client base. The goal was to uncover demographic patterns, enrollment sources, and carrier performance to guide marketing and operational decisions.

Key Questions

  • Where are clients concentrated geographically?
  • What are the age, gender, and Medicaid coverage profiles of the book of business?
  • Which carriers dominate enrollments?
  • What channels (phone vs. in-office) are used most?
  • How do election periods and SEP reasons contribute to enrollments?
  • What are the month-to-month application patterns?

Data & Tools

  • Source: Internal CRM client records (anonymized for portfolio)
  • Tool: Power BI Desktop
  • Process: Data cleaning, aggregation, model building, and interactive visualization

Findings · Page 1 (Demographics)

  • State distribution: Highest counts in Indiana (30), Florida (29), Texas (18), Kentucky (14). Total clients: 239.
  • Age distribution: Clients span 50–100+, concentrated between 60–80.
  • Gender: ~67% male, ~33% female.
  • Medicaid status: 69 true vs. 170 false.

Findings · Page 2 (Carriers, Channels, Election Periods, Month Trend)

  • Carrier mix: Humana, UHC, Devoted, Aetna, Anthem, Cigna, and WellCare are all represented.
  • Sales channel: Phone ≈ 94.5% vs. In-office ≈ 5.5%.
  • New to MA: Mix of new vs. returning enrollees.
  • Election periods: Applications span AEP, OEP, SEP, IEP, ICEP, IEP2, and MCD.
  • SEP “Used by” reasons: Categories include MDE, CDC, LEC, CSN, DST, 5ST, MCD, MRD, OCC.
  • Month trend: Applications by month (2024–2025) with carrier breakdown.

Business Impact

  • Targeting: Geographic concentration guided where seminars and mailers should be focused.
  • Operations: The 95% phone-based sales mix justified prioritizing call-center resources and training.
  • Compliance & product strategy: Carrier and election-period data informed licensing and compliant outreach timing.
  • Planning: Month-level application patterns supported pacing of marketing and staffing, especially around AEP and OEP.

Limitations

  • Based on a snapshot of 239 clients; patterns may shift with larger samples.
  • Data anonymized for privacy, so only aggregated results are shown here.

Next Steps

  • Add ROI metrics linking marketing spend to enrollments.
  • Enable drill-through to client segments (age + state + carrier).
  • Incorporate year-over-year trend lines for clearer season comparisons.

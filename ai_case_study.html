Case Study · AI R&D Spending & Stock Impact (Tableau)

Data privacy: Uses synthetic/market-style financial data to demonstrate methods without exposing confidential information.

Overview

This dashboard examines relationships between company R&D spending, AI revenue, and stock performance over time, with annotations for notable AI events. The objective was to connect investment patterns to efficiency and market reaction in a single, readable view.

Key Questions

  • How have R&D expenditures evolved by company?
  • Are companies becoming more efficient (spending relative to revenue)?
  • When do AI revenues approach or surpass R&D spend?
  • Which product/feature releases align with the largest stock moves?
  • How do companies differ in index changes through time?

Data & Tools

  • Source: Synthetic financial & event dataset (company-level R&D, AI revenue, stock index, event log)
  • Tool: Tableau
  • Process: Data integration, calculated fields for ratios, dual‑axis line charts, and event annotations

Findings

  • R&D growth: Spend rises steadily across all three companies, with Google & Meta growing fastest (top‑left chart).
  • Efficiency trend: Spending/Revenue ratios decline for all companies over the period, indicating improving efficiency; one firm remains structurally higher‑cost (top‑middle).
  • Revenue vs. spend: AI revenue lines trend upward and approach/overtake R&D spend for Google & Meta around 2022–2023 (top‑right).
  • Event impacts: Releases such as AI‑powered search and image‑generation models align with the strongest positive stock impacts (bottom‑left bar summary).
  • Index changes: Company index lines show distinct volatility profiles; spikes coincide with the noted product events (bottom‑right line chart).

Business Impact

  • Investor lens: Clarifies how sustained R&D can transition toward profitability in AI lines of business.
  • Strategy: Identifies phases where incremental R&D produces outsized revenue growth and market response.
  • Communication: Event annotations help explain why certain quarters outperformed, improving stakeholder narratives.

Limitations

  • Illustrative, synthetic data; results show technique, not investment advice.
  • Macroeconomic variables (rates, liquidity, risk appetite) not modeled.

Next Steps

  • Extend with predictive models estimating expected stock impact for scheduled releases.
  • Add competitor & sector benchmarks to contextualize efficiency and returns.
  • Drill down by product line/region; add tooltips linking to event sources.
